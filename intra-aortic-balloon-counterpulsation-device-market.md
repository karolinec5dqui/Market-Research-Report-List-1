<p><p>The Intra-aortic Balloon Counterpulsation Device (IABP) is a mechanical device used to enhance coronary blood flow and reduce the workload on the heart, primarily during critical cardiac conditions like heart failure or myocardial infarction. The device works by inflating and deflating a balloon placed in the descending aorta, synchronized with the cardiac cycle, thus improving myocardial perfusion and aiding cardiac output.</p><p>The market for IABP devices is witnessing significant growth, driven by the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, advancements in technology, and a rising geriatric population. The focus on improving patient outcomes during high-risk procedures and the growing number of cardiac surgeries are propelling demand for IABP systems.

<p><ul><li>Alkaline</li><li>Acidic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Inert resins in the market are categorized primarily into alkaline and acidic types based on their pH response and chemical interactions. Alkaline inert resins are designed to provide a basic environment, often used in applications requiring enhanced stability and resistance to acid decomposition. Conversely, acidic inert resins facilitate processes that benefit from a low pH, often promoting reactions that are unfavorable in neutral or alkaline conditions. Both types play crucial roles in industries such as chromatography and material separation.</p></p>

<p><p>Membrane technology in the pharmaceutical market encompasses various filtration processes to separate or concentrate substances. Microfiltration utilizes porous membranes to remove larger particles, while ultrafiltration can separate macromolecules based on size. Nanofiltration is used for smaller solutes, selectively allowing specific ions and small molecules to pass. Reverse osmosis provides a high level of purification, by removing dissolved salts and contaminants. Other membrane technologies may include advanced filtration methods like electrofiltration and membrane distillation, enhancing efficiency and product quality in pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
